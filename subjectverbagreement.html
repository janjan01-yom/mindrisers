SUBJECT-VERB AGREEMENT

The verb must always agree with the SUBJECT in number (singular or plural) and in person. Use a singular verb with a singular subject and a plural verb with a plural subject.
  • 1. Abstract and mass nouns use a singular verb.

Ex. Water is essential.

       Health is wealth.

  • 2. A collective noun takes a singular verb if it is thought as a whole, but require plural verb if it is thought as individuals composing it.

Ex. The team gets the highest score.

      The team are saddened by their own grades.

  • 3. Not all nouns that end in –s are plural. Some nouns that end in –s are singular in meaning, and therefore require a singular verb.

Ex. Philippines, Mathematics, Physics

  • 4. Nouns that end in –s that are composed of two parts use a plural verb.

Ex. scissors, pants, trousers, binoculars

  • 5. Some indefinite pronouns are always singular, such as the words every, much, little, no one, each and those that end with –body, -one, -thing (ex. anybody, someone, everything).

Ex. Everyone is expected to attend the event.

      Nobody likes to be judged negatively.

      Much is expected of you.

  • 6. Some indefinite pronouns are always plural, such as the words few, several, many, others, two, both, pairs of, kinds of, etc.

Ex. Many still believe in God.

  • 7. The indefinite pronouns either and neither use a singular verb.

Ex. Either of the twins is qualified.

      Neither was invited.

  • 8. The relative pronouns who, which, and that should have the same number as the nouns they refer to.

Ex. You who are my friends love me.

  • 9. A number takes a plural verb; The number takes a singular verb.

Ex. A number of students are taking the exam.

      The number of scouts joining the parade is small.

  • 10. When an adjective is used as a noun referring to people, it is plural.

Ex. The meek are favored by God.

  • 11. Titles of books, movies, etc. takes a singular verb.

Ex. Tom and Jerry is a famous cartoon.

  • 12. Weight, height, quantity take a singular verb.

Ex. Twenty miles is quite a long distance to run.

      Five pesos starts the video game machine.

  • 13. Intervening words such as together with, as well as, along with, including, etc., do not change the number of the subject.

Ex. Samantha, together with her friends, is going to the party.

      Mom and dad, as well as George, are not feeling well.
