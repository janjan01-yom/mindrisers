PLANT AND ANIMAL CELL

CELL THEORY
  • Cell is the basic unit of structure and function in organisms.
  • All functions necessary for the survival of an organism are ultimately performed by cells.
  • All living organisms are made up of one or more cells.
  • UNICELLULAR - made up of one cell.
  • MULTICELLULAR - made up of more than one cell.
  • All cells arise from pre-existing cells.

CELL ANATOMY

Cell Membrane/Plasma Membrane

Semi permeable membrane enclosing the cell separating the internal environment from the outside and serves as the location of chemical exchange for many life functions.

Cytoplasm

Internal environment of the cell; contains all of the internal organelles and the fluid medium they are suspended in.

Nucleus

Central storage of genetic material of the cell.

Mitochondrion

Considered as the 'powerhouse' of the cell; produces energy for use of the organism.

Lysosome

Site for internal digestion of food chemicals.

Endoplasmic Reticulum

Site of lipid and protein synthesis.

Golgi Bodies/Apparatus

Site for modification, sorting and storing of proteins.

Ribosome

Necessary organelle for protein synthesis.

Vacuole

Storage site for food and other chemicals; can be used for other purposes

Centriole

Accessory organelle for cell division.

ANIMAL CELL

PLANTCELL

CELLULAR PARTS THAT ARE ONLY PRESENT IN PLANTS AND SOME OTHER ORGANISMS:
  •  Cell wall  - Rigid membrane for additional support and structure.
  • Plastids - Specialized organelles that perform specific special functions.
  • Chloroplasts - Responsible for performing photosynthesis; usually contain green pigment.
  • Chromoplast - Enhances reproduction and propagation of plants.
  • Amyloplast - Storage of starch and sugar; usually colorless.
