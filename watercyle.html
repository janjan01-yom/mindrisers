WATER CYCLE

The water cycle describes how water evaporates from the surface of the earth, rises into the atmosphere, cools and condenses into rain or snow in clouds, and falls again to the surface as precipitation. The cycling of water in and out of the atmosphere is a significant aspect of the weather patterns on Earth.

Evaporation

Heat from the sun evaporated from oceans, lakes, moist soil, leaves and bodies of animals and humans.

Condensation

Water molecules liquify and cling to dust particles in the high air to form clouds.

Precipitation

When moisture accumulating in the air becomes too heavy, they fall back to the earth; can be in the form of rain, snow, or hail.
