FIGURES OF SPEECH

Figure of speech is a phrase or a word that has a different meaning than its literal meaning. It conveys meaning by identifying or comparing one thing to another, which has a connotation or meaning familiar to the audience or reader.

SIMILE 

A stated comparison between two things that are actually unlike but have something in common. It is introduced by the words like, as, resemble or similar to.

Example: Our soldiers are as brave as lions. 

METAPHOR

Makes a direct comparison of two unlike things that have something in common. It does not include words like, as, resemble or similar to.

Example: His cotton candy words did not appeal to her taste.  

PERSONIFICATION

Gives human qualities to an object, an animal, or an idea.

Example: Mary heard the last piece of cake calling her name. 

APOSTROPHE

An apostrophe addresses personified objects as real persons, the absent as if they were present, and the dead as if they were alive.

Example: “Time, you old gypsy man, will you not stay?” 

ALLUSION

Allusion is a reference in a work of literature, or to a well-known person, place or event outside of literature.

Example: Victor is the Adonis of his class. 

HYPERBOLE

A hyperbole exaggerates an idea so vividly that the reader has an instant picture.

Example: I am so hungry I could eat a horse. 

RHETORICAL QUESTION

A rhetorical question is a question to which the speaker expects no spoken answer but hopes for the mental one that he forcefully suggests.

Example: “O Wind, if Winter comes, can Spring be far behind?” 

OXYMORON

An oxymoron juxtaposes elements that appear to be contradictory, but which contain a concealed point.

Example: James Bond is a well-known secret agent. 

PARADOX

A paradox is a seemingly contradictory but true statement.

Example: Attack is the best form of defense. 

CHIASMUS

A chiasmus is parallelism in which words, grammatical constructions, or concepts are repeated in reverse prder, in the same, or a modified form.

Example: Never let a fool kiss you or a kiss fool you. 

METONYMY

Metonymy is the substitution of one noun for another which it suggests. It is based on association (e.g. the author of his works, the source for the product, the cause for the effect)

Example: Malacanang declared the suspension of classes. 

SYNECDOCHE

Synecdoche is a type of metonymy in which a significant part is used to represent the whole.

Example: It is pretty useless to preach to my empty stomachs.
